    Lil Baby

    Dominique Armani Jones, who goes by the stage name Lil Baby, is an Atlanta-based rapper, singer, and songwriter. His signature flow, storytelling, and melodic delivery are showcased in songs like "Drip Too Hard", "The Bigger Picture", and "Freestyle". He's known for his ability to portray the complexities of street life and personal growth in his verses. Lil Baby's debut album, Harder Than Ever, featured the hit "Yes Indeed" with Drake.

    Heading 2

    Life growing up
    Lil Baby, born Dominique Jones in 1994, grew up in the tough Oakland City neighborhood of Southwest Atlanta, Georgia. Raised by a single mother alongside two sisters after his father left when he was just two years old. As he got older, his life moved toward crime and drug dealing. He eventually dropped out of Booker T. Washington High School in the ninth grade to focus on street hustling. His path in the streets resulted in multiple legal troubles. By the time he was a teenager, he was in and out of trouble, which ultimately landed him a two-year sentence in the penitentiary.

    Getting into music
    It was actually his reputation as a young hustler and gambler that eventually saved him. Because he already earned the respect of the local rap community, figures like Kevin "Coach K" Lee and Young Thug pushed him to channel his hustle into the recording studio. After getting out of prison, he launched his wildly successful rap career in 2017 with the song "My Dawg" to provide a different life for his children than the one he experienced growing up.
