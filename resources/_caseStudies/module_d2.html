Sunday, 27th February, 2005 – Saturday, 5th March, 2005

Date: Mon, 28 Feb 2005 15:24:33 -0500 (EST)

Subject: No meeting this week

From: Hank

To: crane-cs-l@mtu.edu

Denise,

 I'm out of town this week, but, I can check my email.

 I can't meet instead please send me a short update,  and any questions you folks have. Also, please let the other people know (including the monitor) that we won't be meeting.

 Thanks,

-- Hank

So Denise replies:

Date: Tue, 1 Mar 2005 17:24:33 -0500 (EST)

Subject: Re: No meeting this week

From: Denise

To: Hank

Cc: crane-cs-l@mtu.edu

Hank,

We are continuing code decomposition and design of the MatLab model this week. We met Sunday evening to discuss how to implement the new PCS code, including deciding which functions we felt were no longer necessary. We also cleared up some design ideas about MatLab and Bob gave us his report from the meeting he had with the platform team on Thursday. Sounds like the platform team is going to be behind schedule, which may impact our real-world testing, and they also do not have any better idea of the ranges of inputs that we will be taking in in our program.

This leads to some questions about the code in the main function, since

that code not only takes in the serial data about the current movement and crane state, it also does some calculations on it in functions like

EstimateShipMotion, Estimate_Slope, and FilterShipMotion. Will the data we are taking in going to be the same values that this old program took in, so we will need to do the same calculations? Or will we be getting data that is already estimated, filtered, etc?

At the end of the while loop in the main function there is a call to

data_log, which writes the current state and operations to a log on

separate machine. Are we keeping that in?

We have also generated some questions about implementation in MatLab, such as in all of the simulations we have looked at so far we give a specified run time length, how will the new program continue executing as long as the user's wish it to?

The team is meeting on Thursday to do a similar re-design of CONTROL1, so I am sure we will have many more questions for you after that.

We will see you for the next meeting on March 15,

--Denise

Questions:

  1. Denise sends an email meeting reminder to the team about the next meeting, and an update to Hank on the team's progress. She later takes verbal updates from the team during their internal meeting.
  • Why do you suppose Hank asked Denise directly for a team progress update and not any other member of the team?
  • Should Denise have had to send out a meeting reminder to the rest of the team members? Do you suppose it had something to do with attendance issues in the past or was it just good practice?
  • Are these duties appointed to Denise, or are they things that Denise had taken on, on her own?
  1. At Week 5, the Seabase II team is holding internal meetings and working on understanding the intricacies of the code. Compare with Seabase I and see where the team was at this point, chronologically. What do you think is the primary difference in approach?
  2. What type of work do you think internal team meetings are good for?Do you think brainstorming is a common and useful approach in design projects
  • Do all members appear equally involved? Are all members just as equally active in front of Hank as they are in the internal meetings? What do you suppose is the reason for that?
